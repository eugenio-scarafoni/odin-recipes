Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti with Carbonara sauce is a traditional Italian dish. It is said the first "Carbonara" was made in the 1940s in a trattoria in Rome but the origins of this recipe are not totally clear. The most reliable story in fact relates the encounter between the ingredients available to the American soldiers and the imagination of a Roman cook. This prototype of "Carbonara" was made with eggs, bacon and cheese. Gradually the recipe evolved to the one we all know and love today!

The "Carbonara" sauce is easy to make and it can be prepared in just a few minutes. You will need just a few ingredients and follow these simple steps to prepare a tasty dinner!

Ingredients (for 4 servings)

  • 320 g spaghetti
  • 150 g guanciale
  • 50g Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 6 egg yolks
  • black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Put a pot of salted water on the burner
  2. Cut the guanciale into slices , then into strips 1 cm thick
  3. Put the guanciale strips into a nonstick pan and brown them for about 15 minutes
  4. Put the spaghetti in the boiling water and cook for the time indicated on the package
  5. Pour the egg yolks in a bowl, add most of the Pecorino cheese and black pepper and whip by hand
  6. Add a tablespoon of cooking water to diluite the mixture and stir
  7. Drain the pasta into the pan with guanciale and stir it
  8. Remove from heat and pour the mixture of eggs and cheese into the pan and mix quickly
  9. Serve immediately the remaining Pecorino cheese and a pinch of black pepper on top
